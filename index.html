Common Cyber Attacks and How to Prevent Them

Types of Attacks

  • Phishing: Fake emails or websites trick users into sharing sensitive information.
  • Man-in-the-Middle Attack (MITM): An attacker intercepts communication between two parties to steal or manipulate data.
  • Malware: Malicious software designed to damage or gain unauthorized access to devices.
  • DDoS (Distributed Denial of Service): Attackers flood a website/server with traffic to disrupt service.
  • Ransomware: Hackers encrypt your data and demand payment to unlock it.
  • Brute Force Attacks: Attackers use trial-and-error methods to guess passwords or PINs.

How to Protect Yourself

  • Enable Two-Factor Authentication: Add an extra layer of security by requiring a second verification step.
  • Update Software Regularly: Always keep your operating systems, browsers, and antivirus software updated.
  • Avoid Public Wi-Fi: Use a VPN if you must connect to public networks.
  • Use Strong Passwords: Create passwords that are at least 12 characters long and avoid common words.
  • Backup Your Data: Regularly back up important files in case of a ransomware attack.
